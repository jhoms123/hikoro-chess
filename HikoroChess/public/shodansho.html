Rules of Sho Dan Sho

Set Up & Turn Structure

First Turn: Each player drops a piece from their hand onto ANY starting gate. Subsequent drops must be on friendly-controlled fields or ANY starting gate.

Normal Turns: You may move or drop ONE piece per turn.

Harmony & Disharmony

Harmony: A closed loop of at least 4 friendly pieces where every piece harmonizes with the pieces it is connected to (having uninterrupted line of sight). Displayed as bright blue dotted lines. When 4+ pieces successfully form a closed structure, the area inside becomes a protective Harmony Zone.

  • Harmony Pairs: Star (Water, Sun, Daisy, Chysalith). | Sun (Star, Shining, Clover, Chysalith). | Clover (Sun, Daisy, Water, Chysalith). | Daisy (Clover, Shining, Star, Chysalith). | Shining (Sun, Daisy, Water, Chysalith). | Water (Shining, Clover, Star, Chysalith). | Chysalith (All other friendly pieces).
  • If a harmony surrounds the central garden, the player owns it.
  • Enemy pieces trapped inside cannot move, and cannot enter an opponent's harmony.
  • Friendly pieces inside a harmony ignore negative effects (e.g. Suns don't flee Chysaliths, Shinings aren't frozen).

Disharmony: Opposing natural forces. Displayed as bright red dotted lines.

  • Disharmony Pairs: Star (Clover, Shining). | Sun (Water, Daisy). | Clover (Shining, Star). | Daisy (Sun, Water). | Shining (Clover, Star). | Water (Sun, Daisy).
  • If two flowers are disharmonizing, neither can harmonize with any other flower.
  • Chysaliths can never disharmonize but will block enemy pieces from forming harmonys if in view.

Core Mechanics

Movement

  • Pieces move orthogonally across lines, stopping on intersections.
  • Steps are counted by moving to adjacent intersections. Direction can be changed at any intersection mid-move.
  • "In View": Refers to a piece being on the same unbroken line as a target, with no other pieces blocking the line of sight between them.
  • Skip Turn (Double-click): Any piece with an even number of steps (Chysalith, Clover, Water, Sun) can simply move out and back to skip its turn by double clicking itself.

Capturing & Dropping

  • Landing exactly on an enemy piece captures it. The moving piece stops there.
  • Dropping restrictions: Pieces from the hand can only be dropped in ANY Starting Gate OR within a Field Zone.
  • To drop in a Field Zone, you must have at least one piece already there, AND the number of your Chysaliths touching that field must exceed any other single opponent's Chysaliths.

Winning Conditions

Control the Center Garden. You can do this by either:

  • Having your pieces occupy all vertices around the Center Garden.
  • Enclosing the Center Garden completely within a Harmony.

Piece Compendium

★

Star

Count: 4

Moves any number of intersections in a straight line. 1-step limit past rivers (unless edge strictly separates river and field).

♣

Clover

Count: 4 | Steps: 4

If in view of a friendly Clover, it may jump to it and pick a new direction for unlimited steps. Can chain jumps.

✿

Daisy

Count: 6 | Steps: 3

Standard mover. Unaffected by rivers.

✥

Water

Count: 6 | Steps: 2

Must be dropped in a Green field zone and can never leave that specific zone.

☼

Sun

Count: 3 | Steps: 4

Invulnerable (except to enemy Chysaliths) when in the same field or in view of a friendly Chysalith. Cannot capture normal pieces. Stops enemy Shining. Cannot move into enemy Chysalith view.

✺

Shining

Count: 3 | Steps: 3

Chysaliths cannot enter its line-of-sight or share a field with it. Freezes Chysaliths already in its field. Cannot step past enemy Sun view.

C

Chysalith

Count: 3 | Steps: 2

Functions like a normal piece but with special interactions. Restricted by enemy Shining flowers. The only piece that can capture an invulnerable enemy Sun. If in view of an enemy piece, that piece cannot form Harmonies.
