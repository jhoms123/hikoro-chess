Rules of Sho Dan Sho

Set Up & Turn Structure

First Turn (Setup): Each player places their Chysalith flower on any intersection strictly within their designated Starting Gate.

Normal Turns: Every subsequent turn consists of two mandatory actions (in order):

  1. Move or drop a Chysalith flower.
  2. Move, drop, or pick up any other flower (or pass this phase).

Harmony & Disharmony

Harmony: A network of at least 4 friendly pieces with uninterrupted line-of-sight to valid partners. Harmonized pieces project blue dotted lines.

  • Pairs: Star-Water, Star-Sun, Star-Daisy, Sun-Shining, Sun-Clover, Clover-Daisy, Clover-Water, Daisy-Shining, Shining-Water.
  • Ignores negative effects (e.g. Harmonized Suns don't flee Chysaliths, Shining isn't frozen).
  • Traps enemy Chysaliths inside and blocks them from crossing harmony lines.
  • Can automatically capture wild gardens or neutralize enemy gardens it touches.

Disharmony: Opposing natural forces. Displayed as red dotted lines.

  • Pairs: Star-Clover, Star-Shining, Sun-Water, Sun-Daisy, Clover-Shining, Daisy-Water.
  • If two friendly pieces disharmonize, neither can form any Harmonies.

Core Mechanics

Movement

  • Pieces move orthogonally across lines, stopping on intersections.
  • Steps are counted by moving to adjacent intersections. Direction can be changed at any intersection mid-move.
  • "In View": Refers to a piece being on the same unbroken line as a target, with no other pieces blocking the line of sight between them.

Capturing & Dropping

  • Landing exactly on an enemy piece captures it. The moving piece stops there.
  • Dropping restrictions: Pieces from the hand can only be dropped in your Starting Gate OR within a Field Zone.
  • To drop in a Field Zone, you must have at least one piece already there, AND the number of your Chysaliths touching that field must exceed any other single opponent's Chysaliths.

Winning Conditions

Control the corners of the Garden areas (4 small, 1 large central). At least one of your occupying pieces must be a Chysalith.

  • 2 Players: Control Central Garden + 2 small gardens.
  • 3 Players: Control Central Garden + 1 small garden.
  • 4 Players: Control Central Garden only.

Critical: A Chysalith must always be on board. If a player loses all Chysaliths from board and hand, they are eliminated.

Piece Compendium

★

Star

Count: 4

Moves any number of intersections in a straight line. Cannot cross rivers.

♣

Clover

Count: 4 | Steps: 4

If in view of a friendly Clover, it may jump to it and pick a new direction for unlimited steps. Can chain jumps.

✿

Daisy

Count: 6 | Steps: 3

Standard mover. Cannot cross rivers.

✥

Water

Count: 6 | Steps: 2

Must be dropped in a Green field zone and can never leave that specific zone.

☼

Sun

Count: 3 | Steps: 4

Uncapturable and cannot capture. Stops enemy Shining flowers in view. Must be moved or picked up if threatening an enemy Chysalith.

✺

Shining

Count: 3 | Steps: 3

Chysaliths cannot enter its line-of-sight or share a field with it. Freezes Chysaliths already in its field. Paused if in view of enemy Sun.

C

Chysalith

Count: 2 | Steps: 2

Crucial piece required to capture gardens and win. Restricted by enemy Shining flowers. Must prioritize moving if threatened by enemy Shining.
